Casein is basically a Milk Protein. It is the only known source of Animal Protein that found in milk in abundant quantity. It is the principal protein of any milk. It represent 3.5 - 4.0 % of the whole milk and almost 80.0 % of the total protein found in milk. It present in the milk in the form of colloidal particles known as Casein Micelle combined with Calcium Phosphorous Bond. The isoelectric point of Casein is 4.6. It is derived by acid precipitation of skimmed milk. It is insoluble in water and required alkali medium to dissolve.
There are mainly 3 basic types of casein depending on its use.

More About The Product
The casein is traditionally made by the farmers or milkmen at household level in the villages.
The dairy farmers, who are engaged in making ghee by traditional method, also manufacture Buttermilk Casein as its by-product.
The Process
For making Buttermilk Casein, Buttermilk (Mattha) is first heated or it is mixed with Phitkari (Potash Alum) or Tatri (Tartaric Acid) after that entire mattha is coagulated and converted into solid particles of smaller size floating in the water.
Then it puts into muslin cloth for draining the water from coagulum. Now the remaining material is put under sun light for drying on cloth or polythene sheets.
Dried product is called Buttermilk Casein.
The Buttermilk Casein produced in this method is relatively smaller in granules size and of white or light brown in color.
